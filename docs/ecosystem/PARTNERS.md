# Research and Clinical Partners

This document tracks active, prospective, and past partnerships for BSC Lab
and BioSynCare. It is an internal working document, not a public roster.

> **Distinction:** BSC Lab partnerships concern research use of the open
> platform and ontology contributions. BioSynCare partnerships concern the
> commercial product. Both are tracked here because many relationships span
> both layers.

---

## Partnership tiers

**Tier A — Research Collaboration.** Joint research design, data collection
using BSC Lab infrastructure, co-authorship on publications. Requires a data
sharing agreement and IRB/ethics clearance for any human subjects research.

**Tier B — Ontology Contribution.** Institution contributes vocabulary terms,
evidence assessments, or alignments to the SSTIM ontology. Lightweight; no
data sharing required. Contributor credited in SSTIM ontology metadata.

**Tier C — BioSynCare Integration.** BioSynCare is deployed within a clinical
or wellness setting. May involve custom preset design, white-labeling, or
API integration. Commercial arrangement separate from open-source contributions.

**Tier D — Advisory / Observer.** Informal relationship; institution uses
BSC Lab for teaching or internal research without formal contribution agreement.
No IP or data commitment.

### Music therapy research groups (Brazil, Italy)
**Target:** Research groups at UNICAMP Music Department (São Paulo), Conservatorio
di Modena, or similar institutions with interest in rhythmic auditory stimulation.  
**Tier:** B + D  
**Rationale:** Overlap between BSC's Sonic Symmetry technique and music therapy's
use of rhythmic structure. Potential vocabulary contribution on music-therapy
adjacent terms.

### Occupational therapy institutions
**Target:** OT programs with interest in sensory modulation interventions.  
**Tier:** B + D  
**Rationale:** The OT community's "sensory modulation" literature is the most
established adjacent body of work. BSC Lab's approach of designed, parameter-
specified delivery is complementary to OT's focus on self-regulatory capacity.

### Sleep research centers
**Target:** Academic sleep labs interested in delta/theta-targeting protocol
research.  
**Tier:** A  
**Rationale:** BSC's sleep onset presets are among the most evidence-supported
in the catalog. A sleep lab partnership would provide the infrastructure for
controlled polysomnography studies.

## BioSynCare clinical partners (prospective)

These are commercial integration discussions, tracked separately from
research partnerships but recorded here for coordination:

**Wellness centers and spas** interested in deploying BioSynCare as a
guided relaxation service: discussions in Italy (Modena region) and Brazil.

**Corporate wellness programs** interested in Perform-group presets for
sustained focus during knowledge work.

For clinical or medical-adjacent deployments, the following must be
confirmed before proceeding:

- Regulatory status in the target country (wellness vs. medical device classification)
- Appropriate consent and user information requirements
- Insurance and liability considerations
- Coordination with BioSynCare's Terms of Service and privacy policy
