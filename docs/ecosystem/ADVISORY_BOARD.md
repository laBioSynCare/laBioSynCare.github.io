## Role of the advisory board

The advisory board provides:

1. **Evidence review.** Input on evidence tier assignments for Tier 4+ claims,
   particularly for new technique categories and novel evidence interpretations.

2. **Methodology guidance.** Feedback on BSC Lab's evidence framework and
   self-report methodology as Phase 3 research infrastructure is built.

3. **External credibility.** Named academic association with peer-reviewed
   institutions, relevant for grant applications, W3C CG charter, and
   research partnerships.

4. **arXiv and publication review.** Review of defensive publications and
   research papers before submission.

Advisory board members do not have governance authority over the repository,
ontology, or product. Their role is advisory and reviewal.

## Criteria for future members

Advisory board additions should meet at least two of these criteria:

- PhD or equivalent in a relevant field (neuroscience, audiology, cognitive
  psychology, rehabilitation science, signal processing)
- Active publication record in sensory stimulation, brainwave entrainment,
  HRV biofeedback, or adjacent areas
- Institutional affiliation at a recognized research university or hospital
- No financial conflict of interest with BioSynCare (advisory relationships
  are unpaid; any compensation arrangement must be disclosed)

The advisory board should eventually represent at least three countries and
two distinct research traditions (e.g., neuroscience + clinical rehabilitation,
or cognitive psychology + signal processing).

---

## Engagement model

Advisory board members are asked for:

- 1–2 hours per quarter for evidence review tasks
- Review of major ontology vocabulary changes (tier 4+ claim assignments)
- Optional co-authorship on research papers where their contribution warrants it
- Named inclusion in the W3C CG charter and BSC Lab documentation with consent

There is no equity, salary, or required meeting attendance. The relationship
is formalized by a brief email exchange stating the role and consent to public
naming.
